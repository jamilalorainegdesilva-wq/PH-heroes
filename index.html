About Bayaning Pilipino

Bayaning Pilipino is an interactive web game and learning hub made to honor and celebrate the life and legacy of Philippine National Heroes. Dive into stories, trivia, and facts about heroes such as Jose Rizal, Andres Bonifacio, Apolinario Mabini, Melchora Aquino, Francisco Makabulos, Gregorio del Pilar, Marcelo H. del Pilar, Sultan Kudarat, Lapu-Lapu, and more!

Designed with animated Filipino motifs and upbeat music, this site aims to make history fun, engaging, and unforgettable for students and Filipinos of all ages.

Facts About Philippine Heroes

Francisco Makabulos is known as the "Savior of Tarlac" for his decisive role in liberating the province during the Philippine Revolution.
Jose Rizal was a doctor, novelist, and the author of Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo.
Andres Bonifacio is called the "Father of the Philippine Revolution" and founded the Katipunan.
Melchora Aquino, also known as "Tandang Sora," aided Katipuneros and is regarded as the "Mother of the Philippine Revolution."
Apolinario Mabini is "The Sublime Paralytic" and one of the brains of the revolution.
Emilio Aguinaldo was the first President of the Philippines and led the country to its first declaration of independence.
Gabriela Silang was the first female leader of a Filipino movement for independence from Spain.
Antonio Luna was a brilliant general and scientist, known for his fierce discipline.
Lapu-Lapu is celebrated as the first Filipino hero who resisted Spanish colonization.
Marcelo H. del Pilar was known as "Plaridel" and was a leader of the Propaganda Movement.
Gregorio del Pilar was one of the youngest generals in the Philippine Revolution.
Sultan Kudarat led Mindanao's resistance against Spanish conquest in the 17th century.
Modern Filipino Heroes like doctors, nurses, teachers, and OFWs continue to save lives, educate, and support families, shaping the nation's future every day.

Stories of Valor

Francisco Makabulos: Born in Tarlac, Makabulos organized the revolutionary government in Central Luzon.
With unwavering courage, he led his men through fierce battles, earning the title "Savior of Tarlac" and inspiring generations to stand for freedom.
Jose Rizal: A man of many talents, Rizal's writings sparked the flames of revolution.
His novels awakened national pride and hope, and his martyrdom united Filipinos to fight for justice and dignity.
Andres Bonifacio: A self-made hero, Bonifacio led the Katipunan against Spanish oppression.
His leadership and bravery in the face of danger made him a legend—his spirit remains alive in every Filipino fighting for freedom.
Gabriela Silang: After her husband's assassination, she led forces against the Spanish, fighting bravely for Ilocos and for freedom.
Her courage blazed a trail for women in leadership, proving that heroism knows no gender.
Lapu-Lapu: He led Mactan warriors in 1521 and was the first Filipino to resist foreign domination, defeating Ferdinand Magellan.
His victory showed the world the power of unity and the strength of the Filipino spirit.
Sultan Kudarat: Led Mindanao's sultanates against Spanish colonization, uniting various Muslim groups in the resistance.
His wisdom and determination kept the flame of freedom burning in Mindanao for centuries.
Modern Filipino Heroes: Today, heroism is found in the everyday acts of courage and compassion.
Doctors and nurses battle unseen enemies in hospitals, risking their lives for others.
Teachers unlock the minds of the next generation, planting seeds of hope.
Volunteer responders, OFWs, and community leaders uplift lives in times of crisis.
These modern heroes remind us: Anyone can be a Bayani.

Heroic Trivia

Jose Rizal could speak more than 20 languages!
Andres Bonifacio worked as a warehouseman and sold canes and paper fans before becoming a revolutionary leader.
Melchora Aquino was already 84 years old when she helped the Katipuneros.
Francisco Makabulos created his own secret code used to communicate plans with fellow revolutionaries.
Gregorio del Pilar died heroically at age 24 defending Tirad Pass.
Lapu-Lapu is celebrated every April 27, the day of the Battle of Mactan.
Antonio Luna was a scientist and wrote scientific papers before leading troops as a general.
Marcelo H. del Pilar's pen name was "Plaridel".
Many modern Filipino heroes risk their lives daily—frontliners, teachers, and volunteers are celebrated every August as "National Heroes Month."
